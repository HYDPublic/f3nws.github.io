WWDC tends to be a software-focused event, but Apple may be announcing several new devices at its annual Worldwide Developers Conference on June 5. You can watch Apple's live stream of the event when it starts at 1 p.m. Eastern (10 a.m. Pacific) here. There's a remote possibility we'll get a sneak peek of the iPhone 8, but based on recent reports, there's a much better chance we'll see a revamped a new Siri-powered speaker that will compete against Amazon's legion of Alexa speakers, a revamped 10.5-inch iPad Pro and an update to Apple's MacBook lineup.
