Translucent lobsters might look cool, but face it: they just don't make good lobster rolls. Alex Todd of Chebeague Island, Maine, recently caught this very rare creature while on a fishing trip. Translucent lobsters are incredibly challenging to find: chances of nabbing one are quite literally one in a million . "I’ve never seen a white one. This one was translucent with just a hint of blue in it,” Todd told ABC News . According to the Maine Cost Fisherman's Association, the lobster probably suffers from a genetic condition known as leucism.
