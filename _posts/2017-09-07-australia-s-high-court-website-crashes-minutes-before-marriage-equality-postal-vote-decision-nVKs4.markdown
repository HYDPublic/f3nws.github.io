One of the biggest current debates in Australia — around marriage equality — caused a government website to crash on Thursday. The website for Australia's High Court was out of operation just minutes before the court handed down a decision over the legality of the federal government's same-sex marriage postal vote. And it's currently still out of action: The outage came just moments before the High Court found that the government's postal vote is lawful , a green light which will enable the Australian Bureau of Statistics to send out a non-compulsory survey to enrolled Australians, asking the question: “Should the law be changed to allow same-sex couples to marry?” Read more...
