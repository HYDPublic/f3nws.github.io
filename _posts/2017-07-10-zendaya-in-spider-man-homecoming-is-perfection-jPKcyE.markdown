This post contains spoilers for Spider-Man: Homecoming Before Spider-Man: Homecoming , some of Spidey's most indelible cinematic moments have been thanks to his love interests. Sam Raimi's 2002 take on the webslinger gave us that rain-soaked upside-down makeout scene, and Marc Webb's less-than-beloved reboot The Amazing Spider-Man rode on the real-life chemistry between Andrew Garfield and Emma Stone. But that's not the case with the hero's entry into the Marvel Cinematic Universe. Jon Watts' film has something better: Zendaya's Michelle, who is your new weirdo icon. She's an odd, truth-telling teen with a nasty streak, and the movie never apologizes for her quirks.
