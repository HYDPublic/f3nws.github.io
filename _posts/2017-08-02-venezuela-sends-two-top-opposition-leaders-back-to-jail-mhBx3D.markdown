Venezuela's intelligence service hauled two prominent opposition leaders back to prison Tuesday, triggering international outcry as embattled President Nicolas Maduro moved to shore up his power after an election widely denounced as a sham. In a statement, the Supreme Court said protest leader Leopoldo Lopez and Caracas ex-mayor Antonio Ledezma were sent back to prison because they had violated the terms of their house arrest by making political statements. In a video he pre-recorded in case he was sent back to jail, Lopez urged his supporters to keep fighting Maduro's government.
