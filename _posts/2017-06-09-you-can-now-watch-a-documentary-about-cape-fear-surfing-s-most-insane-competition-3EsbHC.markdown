It's been a year since surfers tore through Cape Solander, a.k.a. Cape Fear, in what is arguably the world's most terrifying surfing competition. Taking place on the coast of Sydney, Australia, the competition organised by Red Bull saw the world's best contend with 8- to 12-foot waves and 27 Olympic-sized pools of water breaking on a shallow reef.
