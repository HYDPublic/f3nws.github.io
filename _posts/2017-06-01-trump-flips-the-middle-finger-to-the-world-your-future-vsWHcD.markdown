President Trump, in a bid to keep a major campaign promise, announced Thursday that he is moving to withdraw the U.S. from the landmark Paris Agreement on Climate Change while he tries to miraculously strike a better deal. To young people, climate advocates and foreign leaders, this is a terrible nightmare. To ordinary citizens generally concerned about climate change, but not convinced of the wisdom of this agreement, it may feel like an epic, bizarre case of political whiplash. In Trump's speech, for example, he failed to accurately describe the agreement's provisions, railing against the economic penalties it imposes on the U.S. economy, when in fact the entire agreement is voluntary.
