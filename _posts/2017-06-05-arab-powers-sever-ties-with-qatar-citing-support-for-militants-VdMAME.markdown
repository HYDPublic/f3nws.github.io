By Noah Browning DUBAI (Reuters) - The Arab world's biggest powers cut ties with Qatar on Monday over alleged support for Islamists and Iran, reopening a festering wound two weeks after U.S. President Donald Trump's demand for Muslim states to fight terrorism. Saudi Arabia, Egypt, United Arab Emirates and Bahrain severed diplomatic relations with Qatar in a coordinated move. Qatar, a small peninsular nation of 2.5 million people, denounced the action as predicated on lies about it supporting militants.
