Staff at a Mexican bakery chain in Houston, Texas, used their time wisely after flooding caused by Hurricane Harvey trapped them inside the business for two days. While they were waiting for the eventual rescue that came Monday morning, four workers at El Bolillo Bakery’s South Wayside Drive branch decided to make as many loaves of bread as possible for their community. HuffPost has reached out for more information on precisely how many loaves the staff created, but it’s clear from images the bakery shared on Facebook and Instagram that it was quite the haul.
