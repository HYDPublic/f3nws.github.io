GigabyteÂ is one of the biggest motherboard makers in the world, and we visited the company's factory in Taiwan to find out how they're manufactured. Gigabyte's Nan Ping factory in Pingzhen City, about an hour west of Taipei, is the last remaining motherboard factory in Taiwan. It produces about 400,000 units per month, or about a quarter of Gigabyte's total motherboard output. Robots remove a lot of the most complex work from humans. However, a surprising amount of construction is still done by hand: the insertion of many larger components, for instance, and visual checks of the motherboards.
